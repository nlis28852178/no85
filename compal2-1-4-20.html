A Journey to Remember
戰地記者：正中

I had the expectation of going to a typical training with my team members on the weekend of June 27th. Little that I know, I was going to be shocked by what took place in this camp.

Upon arriving at the site, our coach immediately put everyone out of their comfort zone, making us tread into unfamiliar territory. He took us through several exercises to teach us the importance of understanding your teammates, working together for a common target, and share your rewards once the goal is accomplished. I felt that the method used were very untraditional and making the events very interesting. After completing these events, I had thought there should not be any more challenges going into the second day. Oh man, was I wrong.


The very last exercise on the 2nd day was by far the most physically exhausting and mentally exhausting activity that I have endured in my life. The exercise seems impossible at the beginning from the instructions. As we get thru several trials, our team believed it would take a miracle to pull this off. The coach continued to challenge all the groups when our moral is down and pushed us for a solution. Our spirits were almost reaching breaking point now. However, we didn’t give up, and our teams kept thinking collectively, then finally, we came up with a solution. While it took us a while to execute the plan to solve the problem at hand, it was the most the rewarding feeling in the world.

Looking back at the training, I felt everyone truly appreciates the team work that got us to the end. This training gave us the opportunity to readjust our attitude, to think outside our box, and most importantly of all, to appreciate your coworkers. This training has touched me to be a stronger contributor at work and a better person in life.

We came in to camp as individuals,but I know we left as a team.
